The wild tables

At the edges of the valleys, in warm caverns and beneath the black-pine forests, live beings once thought motionless. They graze, migrate, camouflage themselves and watch over their little stools.

Meet the 9 species
Behaviour

Warm-blooded furniture

The wild table is not an object moved by whim: it is a social, cautious organism, capable of learning, whose legs respond to vibrations in the ground.

Method

Observation from afar

Naturalists never approach a colony at sunrise. At that hour the tables unfold their corners and mistake a camera tripod for a rival.

Myth

The first plank

Old tales claim a plank fell from the sky during a magnetic storm. Where it touched the earth, the first table opened its eyes.

A nature at tabletop height

This site gathers expedition notes, species sheets and historical hypotheses about the living-table populations. The tone is deliberately sober: their silhouette may raise a smile, but their world obeys rules of startling precision.

They are found in limestone gorges, on dry plateaus, along iron-rich rivers, and sometimes in the ruins of abandoned dining rooms.

“When a wild table stops, it is not giving up on life. It is listening to the ground.” — Field notebook, Valley of the Cloths
