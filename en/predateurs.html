Threats

Predators

Massive but slow, the living table has few enemies — but those few are patient.

Three dangers

The steppe termite attacks the legs from below and can bring down an adult in a single night of silent feasting. The woodcutter, a bipedal predator, hunts the oldest, whose weathered wood is worth any treasure.

But the worst threat does not hunt: it is damp. One rainy season too many, and the tops warp, the joints give way. Against it, no flight is possible.

The tight refectory

To defend itself, the herd gathers in a circle, tops turned outward, the stools sheltered at the centre, and unleashes into the night a thunder of creaks that discourages prowlers.

“You do not hunt a table. You wait for it to drop its guard — and the old ones never do.”
