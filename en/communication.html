Language

Communication

The table has no mouth, no cords, no cry. To speak, it uses its wood, its legs and the ground itself.

Its first language is the creak: by working its joints, it shapes long wooden laments. Each herd has its own “accent”, inherited from the wood it is made of.

Then comes drumming — a sharp tap of a leg on the ground — and body language: a tilted top is an invitation, a raised one a threat. The matriarch emits, through her whole body, vibrations so low that the herd feels them through their legs, miles away.

A dictionary of sounds

Six of the most common “words”, synthesised from wood. Lean in — and turn up the volume.
