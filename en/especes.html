A living classification

The 9 species

The Tabulidae family gathers organisms with a load-bearing top, locomotor legs and varied social behaviour.

Their classification is still debated: some naturalists see them as descendants of wood, others as the last witnesses of a mineral biology. The nine species below were recorded in the field and figured from photographs.

Black-pine table

Tabula pinicola

Territorial, moves in short hops and shelters its young stools beneath its top.

Habitat : Conifer forests, acidic soilsDiet : Resin dew, lichen, fallen needles

Low table of the mists

Mensa nebulosa minor

Moves at dawn, nearly invisible when fog settles over the bog.

Habitat : Peat bogs, abandoned parlours, cold banksDiet : Damp moss, chalk dust, water vapour

Great migratory table

Mensa migratoria grandis

Travels in slow lines of thirty to a hundred, guided by the low stars.

Habitat : Steppes, grain plains, wind corridorsDiet : Lost grain, salt, calcareous crumbs

Night bedside table

Tabula noctis vigilans

Active by night; broods its peg-eggs in its drawer and creaks softly to reassure its brood.

Habitat : Dry caves, deserted rooms, dark cliffsDiet : Moths, pollen, fragments of dreams

Folding cliff table

Plica mensa rupestris

Unfolds or closes its top in a flash, then slides down the rock like a flake of shale.

Habitat : Sheer faces, rocky ledgesDiet : Soft minerals, spray, stone-fly eggs

Ceremonial round table

Orbimensa antiqua

Gathers in a circle, each one turning its top toward the centre; the matriarch leads the herd.

Habitat : Megalithic circles, ancient clearingsDiet : Cold ashes, crushed berries, remembered sound

Horned workshop table

Fabermensa robusta

Powerful and solitary, digs shelters with its reinforced corners; coveted by woodcutters.

Habitat : Forgotten sheds, quarries, birch woodsDiet : Sawdust, rusted iron, wood fungi

Slender-legged bistro table

Cafemensa urbana feralis

Highly social; speaks by chiming its top and rotating its feet at dusk.

Habitat : Urban ruins, empty terraces, old stationsDiet : Sugar, rain, breadcrumbs, copper coins

Hanging liana table

Tabula pendula silvestris

Lives clinging to branches; descends to the ground only to lay its stools.

Habitat : Humid canopies, leafy cavesDiet : Sap, insects, spores, moondust

Proposed classification

Kingdom: Mobilia silenciosa · Phylum: Quadrupedia planata · Class: Tabuliformes · Order: Mensales · Family: Tabulidae.

This classification is not final. Tracks in the dust show that some species have four legs, others three, six, or a number that varies with the seasonal moults. Yet every living table is known by its stabilising reflex: faced with danger, it seeks perfect horizontality.
